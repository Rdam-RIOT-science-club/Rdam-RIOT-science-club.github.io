Core Team

Lorenza Dall'Aglio,Elisabet Blok & Bing Xu currently coordinate the R.I.O.T. Science Club in Rotterdam, at Erasmus MC and Erasmus University Rotterdam, while closely collaborating with the R.I.O.T. Science Club at King's College London.

Representatives

Representatives promote the RIOT Science Club and support the Core Team by initializing and executing initiatives. They are also points of contact for people who want advice on open research.

Senior Representatives

Senior Representatives endorse and promote the RIOT Science Club and give advice to the Core Team on how we can develop even further.

KCL Team

Vision

The R.I.O.T. Science Club is a scientific community providing training in Reproducible, Interpretable, Open, and Transparent Science. We promote the accessibility of information and learning to all, be it scientists, clinicians, or the public, and we strive for reproducible, and easily interpretable results, analyses and codes.

Rotterdam R.I.O.T.S.

Founded at King's College London by Dr. Samuel Westwood and colleagues, the R.I.O.T. Science Club was recently brought to Rotterdam by Elisabet Blok and Lorenza Dall'Aglio and the Rotterdam site is funded by the Erasmus MC. The Rotterdam R.I.O.T.S. seminars occur monthly and are open to scientists, students, clinicians, as well as the public. Lectures, practicals, and discussions on how we can do better, clearer, and more reproducible science are given.

Collaborations

Promoting and teaching open research practices remains a team effort. Thus we collaborate with partners within and outside the Netherlands.
