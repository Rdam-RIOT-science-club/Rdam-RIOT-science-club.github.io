Vision

The R.I.O.T. Science Club is a scientific community providing training in Reproducible, Interpretable, Open, and Transparent Science. We have three core values, being (i) the accessibility of information and learning to all, be it scientists, clinicians, or the public, (ii) striving for reproducible, and easily interpretable results, analyses and codes, and (iii) the use of FAIR as guiding principles.

Rotterdam R.I.O.T.S.

Founded at King's College London by Dr. Samuel Westwood and colleagues, the R.I.O.T. Science Club was recently brought to Rotterdam by Elisabet Blok and Lorenza Dall'Aglio. The Rotterdam R.I.O.T. Science Club is now part of both the R.I.O.T.S. at KCL and of the Open Science Community Rotterdam (OSCR). The Rotterdam R.I.O.T.S. seminars occur monthly and are open to scientists, students, clinicians, as well as the public. Lectures, practicals, and discussions on how we can do better, clearer, and more reproducible science are given.
